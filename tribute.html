Sripathi Panditaradhyula Balasubrahmanyam (S.P.B.)

(4 June 1946 - 25 September 2020)

 

    TIMELINE

  • S. P. Balasubrahmanyam debuted as a playback singer on 15 December 1966 with the Telugu film Sri Sri Sri Maryada Ramanna scored by his mentor, S. P. Kodandapani.

  • In career spanning over five decades, he has won six National Film Awards for Best Male Playback Singer for his works in four different languages â€“ Telugu, Tamil, Kannada, and Hindi;

  • 25 Andhra Pradesh state Nandi Awards for his work in Telugu cinema; and numerous other state awards from Karnataka and Tamil Nadu governments.

  • he won six Filmfare Awards South and a Filmfare Award

  • he held the Guinness World Record for recording the highest number of songs by a singer with over 50,000 songs in 16 languages.

  • On 8 February 1981, he created a record by recording 27 songs in Kannada from 9 am to 9 pm.

  • he recorded 19 songs in Tamil, and 16 songs in Hindi in a day, which has also been called a record.

  • In2012 , he received the state NTR National Award for his contributions to Indian cinema.

  • In 2015, he received the Harivarasanam Award from the Government of Kerala.

  • In 2016, he was honoured with the Indian Film Personality of the Year award at the 47th International Film Festival of India.

  • He was a recipient of the Padma Shri (2001), Padma Bhushan (2011), and Padma Vibhushan (posthumously) (2021) from the Government of India.

  • On 25 September 2020, S. P. Balasubrahmanyam died in Chennai after being hospitalized for over a month for complications due to COVID-19.
